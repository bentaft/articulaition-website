üîÑ Brain Synchronization

Recent neuroscience research confirms that active listening engages brain regions associated with empathy, emotional regulation, and social connection. When individuals feel heard, their reward systems activate, making the experience intrinsically positive.

Brain imaging also shows that being listened to triggers synchrony in areas linked to emotional understanding and perspective-taking. This neural alignment explains why people often feel calmer, more connected, and more open when they are truly listened to.
