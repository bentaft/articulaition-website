PR Professional Lexicon

20 Essential Terms for Public Relations Mastery

Master the specialized vocabulary that defines elite-level public relations and corporate communications practice.

1. Boilerplate

A standardized paragraph at the end of a press release that provides a concise description of the company, its mission, and what it does.

Ensures consistent brand identity and provides essential context for journalists and readers across all official communications. Should be updated quarterly to reflect current positioning.

2. Embargo

An agreement with media outlets to withhold the publication of specific news until a designated date and time.

A high-stakes tactic used to coordinate and maximize the impact of a major announcement, such as a product launch or merger. Builds journalist trust when honored consistently.

3. Earned Media

Unpaid, third-party coverage of a brand, such as a news article, review, or social media mention, generated through PR efforts rather than advertising.

Considered the gold standard in PR because it carries higher credibility and authenticity than paid media. Measured through media monitoring and sentiment analysis.

4. Share of Voice (SOV)

A metric that quantifies a brand's media presence or visibility in comparison to its direct competitors within a specific timeframe or market.

Used to benchmark campaign effectiveness and assess competitive standing, moving beyond simple mention counts to provide strategic market context and ROI measurement.

5. Crisis Communication

The specialized function of protecting and defending an organization's reputation during a public challenge, involving proactive planning, rapid response, and transparent messaging.

A critical capability that requires pre-approved statements, designated spokespeople, and real-time monitoring to control the narrative and mitigate damage to trust and brand equity.

6. Key Messages

Core strategic communications that capture the essential points an organization wants to convey about itself, its products, or its positions on important issues.

These foundational messages serve as the North Star for all communications activities, ensuring consistency across spokespeople, channels, and time periods while resonating with target audiences.

7. Media Relations

The practice of building and maintaining professional relationships with journalists, editors, and media outlets to secure positive coverage and manage information flow.

Success depends on understanding journalist needs, providing valuable story angles, being responsive and reliable, and maintaining relationships even during quiet periods.

8. Stakeholder Mapping

A strategic process of identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing all individuals, groups, or organizations that can affect or are affected by a company's actions and decisions.

Essential for developing targeted communication strategies, understanding influence networks, and ensuring no critical audiences are overlooked in campaign planning and execution.

9. Thought Leadership

The strategic positioning of executives or subject matter experts as authoritative voices in their industry through content creation, speaking opportunities, and media commentary.

Builds long-term brand equity and market influence by demonstrating expertise, driving industry conversations, and establishing trust with key stakeholders and potential customers.

10. Message Discipline

The consistent delivery of predetermined key messages across all communications opportunities, regardless of the specific question asked or situation encountered.

Critical for maintaining narrative control and ensuring strategic messages penetrate target audiences. Requires extensive media training and regular practice to execute effectively.

11. Newsjacking

The practice of inserting your brand or organization into breaking news stories or trending topics to gain media attention and amplify your message.

Requires real-time monitoring, rapid response capabilities, and careful judgment to ensure relevance and appropriateness. Most effective when there's a genuine connection to the story.

12. Reputation Management

The strategic practice of influencing and controlling an organization's reputation through monitoring, analyzing, and responding to public perceptions and conversations.

Encompasses both proactive reputation building through positive storytelling and reactive damage control through crisis response and issue management strategies.

13. Backgrounder

A detailed informational document that provides journalists with comprehensive context, history, and technical details about a company, product, or issue.

Serves as a reference tool for reporters writing in-depth stories, helping them understand complex topics and ensuring accurate, informed coverage of your organization.

14. Soundbite

A brief, memorable, and quotable statement designed to capture the essence of a key message in a format suitable for broadcast media or social sharing.

Must be concise (typically 10-15 seconds), clear, and compelling. Often prepared in advance for media interviews to ensure consistent message delivery across platforms.

15. Media Advisory

A brief notification sent to media outlets to alert them about an upcoming news event, such as a press conference, announcement, or photo opportunity.

Typically includes the who, what, when, where, and why of an event. Used to generate media attendance and coverage for time-sensitive or breaking news situations.

16. Spokesperson Training

Specialized coaching to prepare executives and designated representatives for media interviews, public speaking, and crisis communication situations.

Covers message development, delivery techniques, difficult question handling, and non-verbal communication. Essential for maintaining consistent, professional representation.

17. Editorial Calendar

A strategic planning tool that outlines upcoming publication themes, special issues, and editorial focuses for media outlets throughout the year.

Used to identify optimal timing for pitches and story angles that align with publication needs. Helps PR professionals plan campaigns around relevant editorial opportunities.

18. Influencer Outreach

The strategic process of identifying, engaging, and collaborating with individuals who have significant influence over your target audience's opinions and behaviors.

Extends beyond traditional celebrity endorsements to include industry experts, bloggers, and social media personalities who can authentically advocate for your brand or message.

19. Issue Management

The proactive identification, monitoring, and strategic response to emerging issues that could potentially impact an organization's reputation or operations.

Involves early warning systems, stakeholder analysis, and prepared response strategies to address issues before they escalate into full-scale crises requiring reactive management.

20. Attribution

The practice of clearly identifying sources and quotes in media coverage, including different levels such as on-the-record, off-the-record, and background information.

Critical for building journalist trust and managing information flow. Understanding attribution rules helps PR professionals strategically share information while protecting sensitive details.
